Planning of Water Pump System with Energy Sources of Solar Power Plant in Kampung Jalak Bali, Tengkudak Village, Penebel.

Project Leadership Teamwork Sustainable Problem Solving
Situation

Kampung Jalak Bali in Tengkudak Village relies on a PLN grid-powered water pump system to meet the clean water needs of 293 residents. Full dependence on the grid creates high vulnerability to power outages, electricity tariff fluctuations, and continuously rising operational costs.

Task

To design a solar-powered water pumping system as an efficient, eco-friendly, and sustainable alternative energy source. To conduct a comprehensive technical feasibility study and compare the long-term economic viability between the proposed solar system (PATS) and the existing grid-powered system.

Action

Analysed local solar radiation data using the Global Solar Atlas, identifying an average solar intensity of 4.7 kWh/m2/day at the target location. Planned a battery-less solar water pumping system using a submersible DC pump and an 1,800 Wp solar array capacity 4 x 450 Wp in a 2S2P configuration. Calculated and compared financial performance indicators for both systems, utilizing key investment parameters: Net Present Value (NPV), Benefit-Cost Ratio (BCR), Internal Rate of Return (IRR), and Payback Period.

Result

The finalized design successfully meets the community's demand with a daily water production capacity of ± 30m3/day, backed by a ± 53.9m3 effective storage reservoir. Economic analysis proved the solar system is significantly superior, achieving an NPV of IDR 72,621,334.84 (at a 10% discount rate), an IRR of 14%, and lowering water costs to IDR 2,992.23/m3 outperforming the existing grid system's metrics. The solar powered system demonstrated strong long-term economic resilience for the village through minimized operational expenses and a highly stable cash flow.
